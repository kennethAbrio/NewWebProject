Our History

By the 16th century, coffee had reached the Ottoman Empire, where it continued to grow in popularity. Coffee houses emerged in cities like Istanbul, becoming important social institutions where people from different backgrounds could gather and exchange ideas. Coffee was regarded as a stimulant that aided intellectual and spiritual pursuits. Legend has it that a goat herder named Kaldi noticed his goats becoming energized and restless after eating the bright red berries from a certain shrub. Curious, he decided to try the berries himself and experienced a similar invigorating effect. This discovery led to the cultivation and consumption of coffee.
